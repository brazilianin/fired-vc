The Manifesto

The best solutions to industry problems will not come from 23-year-olds with pitch decks. They will come from the 45-year-old VP who has spent two decades watching the same broken process waste millions, who has designed the fix in her head a hundred times, and who just needs someone to hand her the tools and say:

“Go build it.”

The future of venture isn’t more SAFEs and more pitch competitions. It’s more revenue, more dividends, and more founders who own the companies they were always meant to create.

The Thesis

Three forces are collapsing the old venture model — at the same time.

01

The Three-Person Unicorn

A single founder with an agentic stack ships what previously required a 20-person engineering team. Mac Mini–class hardware can run 10–20 AI agents — an entire org on a desk. Capital requirements drop an order of magnitude; time-to-revenue compresses from years to weeks.

02

The Operator’s Edge

The deepest industry knowledge lives inside the heads of frustrated VPs and Directors — not in startup pitches. Our thesis: the majority of the next wave of unicorns will come from senior domain experts leaving their fields, not from college kids with bold ideas.

03

Revenue, Not Exits

Agentic businesses reach profitability in months. Forcing them onto the SAFE / exit treadmill destroys the very qualities that make them valuable. The right return mechanism is dividends — real estate economics for software.

The Screenless Doctrine

The next decade of software gives attention back.

The last decade was built to capture your attention. The next decade — the agentic decade — is built to give it back. The most powerful Fired.VC products are screenless: agents that make the calls, send the emails, file the docs, and negotiate the deals. No dashboards. No onboarding. The output speaks for itself.

That invisibility is itself the distribution channel. No UI training. No behavioral change. The agent just starts working — and the artifact (the report, the filing, the reply) gets forwarded inside the customer’s org for free.

How the dividend works

  • $50–150K per venture covers the 12-week sprint, infra, legal, initial marketing.
  • 30–40% studio stake — split between common (co-founding) and preferred (capital).

Request the LP Brief
Why dividends instead of exits?

Agentic businesses reach profitability in months, not years. Forcing them onto the SAFE / exit treadmill destroys value. Dividends return capital faster, reduce LP risk, and maintain founder alignment — without giving up exit upside.

Can one person really run a company?

This is the core thesis. Multiple agentic startups have already crossed $1M ARR with 1–3 people. A Mac Mini–class machine can run 10–20 agents. Three people + three machines > a traditional 60-person org.

What if a founder goes back to their job?

The 12-week sprint creates irreversible momentum: paying customers by week 8, an autonomously-running product, public commitment. Vesting + 1-yr cliff backs it up.

How is this different from an accelerator?

Accelerators take 5–10% for mentorship. We take 30–40% because we are the co-founder — AI stack, technical architecture, growth design, capital — building alongside the operator for 12 weeks.
